Poblano Chicken Chowder

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 large carrots, peeled and diced in 1/2 inch pieces
  • 2 large onions, peeled and diced in 1/2 inch pieces
  • 5 celery stalks, trimmed and sliced in 1/2 inch pieces
  • 1/8 cup minced garlic
  • 2 to 3 small poblano peppers, stemmed, seeded, and diced in 1/2 inch pieces
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 tablesppon granulated chicken base
  • 3 quarts chicken broth
  • 1/2 bunch cilantro
  • Roux (see below)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red hot sauce
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 pound diced grilled chicken breast

Roux

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter

Soup

Heat oil in large stockpot over medium heat. Add carrots, onions, celery, garlic, poblanos, cumin, white pepper, salt, and thyme. Saute 7 to 8 minutes or until carrots are tender. Add chicken broth and continue to heat.

Roux

Melt butter in large skillet over medium heat. Add flour and stir to combine. Cook, stirring frequently, 3 to 4 minutes to cook flour. Do not allow roux to brown. Add 1 to 3 cups hot liquid from stockpot by cupfuls into roux, whisking continuously to incorporate. Pour back into stockpot, whisking to blend. Stirring frequently, cook 3 to 5 minutes or until mixture begins to thicken. Remove from heat. Stir in hot sauce. Stir in cream and chicken.

Comments

Don't worry about how much chicken broth or bullion, just use water and then add as much bullion as needed to taste. You also don't have to bother to grill the chicken. Just throw it in the soup and let it boil. No cream? No problem. Use less water and stir in milk instead.

Yield: 4 to 5 quarts
